About Us

The Nigerian Navy Secondary School (NNSS) Abeokuta is a distinguished all-boys boarding school located in Abeokuta, Ogun State, Nigeria. Established on March 2, 1991, it was founded as part of the Nigerian Navy's extensive initiative to provide quality education to the offspring of Navy personnel and civilians alike, contributing significantly to national development.

The school is committed to fostering academic achievement, discipline, and leadership development, integrating both national and international educational best practices. It aims to equip students for successful paths in both civilian and military careers.

Academics

NNSS Abeokuta offers a comprehensive curriculum that adheres to the standards set by Nigeria's Ministry of Education. The academic program is designed to provide students with a strong foundation in various subjects, preparing them for higher education and future careers.

Junior Secondary School (JSS)

The JSS curriculum is prevocational and academic, ensuring students acquire fundamental knowledge and skills. All students offer 11 subjects, including:

  • English Studies
  • Mathematics
  • Cultural and Creative Arts (Music, Drama & Arts)
  • One Nigerian Language (Yoruba, Igbo, or Hausa)
  • Basic Science and Technology (Basic Science, Basic Technology, Computer, Physical Health Education)
  • Pre-Vocational Studies (Agric Science & Home Economics)
  • National Values Education (Social Studies & Civic Education)
  • French
  • Business Studies
  • Arabic Language (Optional)
  • IRS/CRS
  • History

Senior Secondary School (SSS)

SSS students are required to offer between 9 and 12 subjects, structured as follows:

  • SSS 1: Minimum of 12 subjects
  • SSS 2: Minimum of 10 subjects
  • SSS 3: 9 compulsory subjects

Class arms are named after Fast Attack Crafts (FACs) in the Nigerian Naval Fleet, such as AGU, AYAM, BARAMA, DAMISA, EKPE, EKUN, OHUE, and SIRI, ensuring effective academic management.

Admissions

Admission into JSS 1 and student transfers at NNSS Abeokuta are exclusively managed by the Naval Headquarters through the Directorate of Naval Education. The process typically involves a centrally coordinated entrance examination.

Application Process:

  1. Purchase Application Form: Forms are usually available online via a digital scratch card on the Directorate of Naval Education's portal (e.g., pay.nnedu.org).
  2. Complete Registration: After purchasing the scratch card, applicants use the provided Serial number and PIN to complete their registration online.
  3. Entrance Examination: Candidates undergo a competitive entrance examination.
  4. Interview Process: Successful candidates from the examination proceed to an interview.
  5. Admission Offer: Successful candidates in the final interview are issued admission letters and acceptance forms directly from the Naval Headquarters.

Specific age requirements apply for JSS 1 admission, and general entry into the Nigerian Navy (a broader context) has additional criteria regarding citizenship, marital status, medical fitness, height, and educational qualifications (WASSCE, GCE O-Level, NECO, NABTEB, OND, or equivalent). A National Identification Number (NIN) is also mandatory.

Student Life & Daily Routine

Life at NNSS Abeokuta is structured to instill discipline, responsibility, and teamwork. As an all-boys boarding school, students live within the school premises, fostering a close-knit community.

Boarding Houses:

Students are allocated to one of four main houses, named after distinguished past Chiefs of Naval Staff and Directors of Naval Education. These houses include:

  • Blue House (Aikhomu house)
  • Green House (Nyako house)
  • Purple House (Koshoni house)
  • Red House (Omatsola house, originally called Kennedy house)

Each house is overseen by a dedicated House Master, ensuring student welfare and discipline.

Typical Daily Routine:

  • 05:30: Muster for daily assignments (chores or early morning drills)
  • 06:30: Breakfast at the dining hall ("galley")
  • 07:30: Parade (flag flying, commandant's messages, march past)
  • 08:45: Lessons begin
  • 14:30: Lessons end, followed by lunch
  • 15:00: Siesta and guard officers patrol
  • 16:00: Afternoon prep (homework and class assignments)
  • 17:00: Student's free time, training, activities, laundry, or labor duties
  • 18:00: Dinner time
  • 19:00: Evening prep
  • 21:30: End of prep
  • 22:00: Lights out for the whole school

Clubs & Societies:

Students are encouraged to join and actively participate in various clubs and societies to enhance their skills and interests. These include:

  • Literary and Debating Society
  • Music Club
  • Home Makers Club
  • Fine Arts Clubs
  • Young Farmers Club
  • Geographical Society
  • French Club
  • STEM Club
  • Sports and Naval Cadet Training
